What in the World is a 'Token'?

A simple explanation for how computers read.

Think of it Like LEGOs

Imagine you want to build a house out of LEGOs. The final house is your sentence. The individual LEGO bricks are the "tokens".

Different AI models are like different LEGO sets. Some have big, simple bricks (Duplo), while others have small, specialized pieces (Technic). They can all build the same house, but they'll use a different number and type of bricks to do it.

So, a "Token" is Not Universal!

As you can see, the same sentence is broken into a different number of pieces by each AI model.

This matters because many AI services charge by the token. Using an AI that needs more "bricks" to understand you can be more expensive, just like buying more LEGOs!
